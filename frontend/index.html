About

What is Payroll Reconciliation?

The process of verifying that every payroll expense in your accounting system exactly matches what was paid — catching posting errors before they compound.

Payroll reconciliation verifies that amounts recorded in your General Ledger (GL) match the amounts in your Payroll Register for each pay period. Any difference — a variance — signals a potential posting error, missing entry, or mis-classification that needs investigation.

This covers all payroll components: gross wages, employer benefits, employee deductions, and payroll taxes — both employee-withheld and employer-paid.

Manual reconciliation is time-consuming and error-prone. This tool automates the entire pipeline: AI reads your files, maps codes to accounts, and surfaces every variance instantly.

User Guide

Step-by-Step Instructions

Everything you need to complete a reconciliation run from start to finish.

1
Enter Client Name & Pay Period
Enter the Client Name and Pay Period in the top navigation bar. Configuration is saved per client, so returning clients load their mapping automatically.
Use consistent client names across runs — history groups entries by exact client name.
2
Upload the GL Report
Navigate to Step 1 and drop your General Ledger export into the GL Report zone. Select the correct sheet if prompted. AI identifies Transaction Source, GL Code, GL Title, and Net Amount columns. Review and confirm.
The GL file must include a Transaction Source column to filter payroll rows from non-payroll entries.
3
Upload the Payroll Register
Upload your MIP Payroll Register in the second upload zone. The tool identifies Pay Code, Code Type, Earnings, Benefits, Deductions, Employee Tax, and Employer Tax columns. Review and confirm the assignments.
4
Configure the Mapping
Navigate to Step 2 · Configuration. Dropdowns auto-populate with GL codes and pay codes from your uploaded files. Set the Amount Column and Code Type for each row, then click Save Configuration.
Use "Load from Files" to auto-generate rows, or the AI Generator to describe your accounts in plain text.
5
Run the Reconciliation
Go to Step 3 · Run & Results. Confirm all three pre-flight items are green, then click Run Reconciliation. Results appear immediately with a full variance summary.
6
Review Results & Download
Explore results across five tabs: Reconciliation, GL & PR Pivot, GL Pivot, PR Pivot, and Unmapped. Scroll to the bottom and click Download Excel to save the complete 6-sheet report.
Unmapped codes appear in the Unmapped tab — add them to Configuration to include them in future runs.
