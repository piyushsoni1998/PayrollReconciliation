Standard Operating Procedure
Reconciliation of the payroll register with the General Ledger
Objective
To ensure that payroll expenses, deductions, benefits, and tax liabilities recorded in the Payroll Register are accurately reflected in the General Ledger (GL).
Data Sources
Payroll Register Report extracted from payroll system / MIP.
General Ledger Report extracted from MIP Accounting Software.
Both reports are extracted and formatted in Excel to facilitate reconciliation.
Payroll Components
The Payroll Register typically categorizes payroll transactions into the following components:
Earnings – Employee wages, salaries, overtime, and other compensation.
Benefits – Employer-paid benefits such as health insurance, retirement contributions, etc.
Deductions – Employee deductions such as insurance premiums, retirement contributions, and other withholdings.
Taxes – Payroll taxes including employer and employee portions.
GL Recording Structure
In the General Ledger, payroll transactions are recorded as follows:
Earnings → Recorded under Payroll Expense GL codes.
Deductions → Recorded under Payroll Liability GL codes.
Benefits → Recorded under Expense GL codes, with corresponding liability accounts where applicable.
Taxes → Recorded under Expense GL codes with related tax liability accounts.
GL Code Structure Variation
The number of GL codes used for payroll accounting may vary from client to client. For example:
Some organizations record all earnings under a single payroll expense GL code.
Others record payroll expenses using multiple GL codes based on pay codes or payroll categories.
Reconciliation Approach
To perform the reconciliation, the payroll code types must first be mapped to the corresponding GL codes used in the accounting system. Once this mapping is established, totals from the Payroll Register can be aligned with the corresponding GL balances.
Steps for Reconciliation – POT Client
Insert a new column at the end of the GL data titled “Reconciliation Mapping” to record mapping comments, and apply a filter to the GL data to display only “PRS” transactions in the Transaction Source column (Figure 1).

Enter the mapping in the newly added “Reconciliation Mapping” column in the GL as follows:
GL Codes 5000, 5001, 6000 – A. Earnings / Gross Wages
GL Codes 5130, 5140 – B. Benefits / Employer Expenses
GL Codes 2126, 2145, 2146, 2147, 2148 – B.1 Benefits / Employer Expenses – Liabilities
GL Codes 2121, 2127, 2132, 2139, 2140, 2141, 2142, 2143 – C. Deductions / Employee Deductions – Liabilities
GL Codes 2115, 2120 – D. Employee & Employer Taxes – Liabilities
GL Code 5100 – E. ER Tax / Employer Taxes
GL Code 1020 – F. Bank Payment to Employee (Net Pay)
